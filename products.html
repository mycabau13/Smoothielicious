PRODUCTS WE USE

Choose the products from supplements to fruits to greens and add-ins.

Supplements

Adding supplements like chia seeds in the smoothie can add extra protein, fiber, antioxidants, amino acids, and other essential nutrients.
Product Benefits
Spirulina Protein, iron, B vitamins, beta-carotene, vitamin E, manganese, zinc, copper, selenium.
Chia Seeds Omega-3 fatty acids, fiber, antioxidants, and calcium.
Matcha Powder Green tea provides a higher concentration of antioxidants. Improves blood flow, lowers cholesterol and prvent high blood pressure.
Cacao Full of antioxidants, magnesium, and provides a boost of caffeine.

Fruits

Fruit smoothies provide vitamins, minerals, and many other beneficial nutrients for your body.
Product Benefits
Bananas They are rich in potassium and fiber. They may help prevent asthma, cancer, high blood pressure, diabetes, cardiovascular disease, and digestive problems
Mangoes A low-calorie fruit that is high in fibre, and is a great source of vitamins A and C. It also contains folate, B6, iron and a little calcium, zinc and vitamin E.
Strawberries A sodium-free, fat-free, cholesterol-free, low-calorie food.
Pineapples A tropical fruits that are rich in vitamins, enzymes and antioxidants. They may help boost the immune system, build strong bones and aid indigestion.
Blueberries The fiber, potassium, folate, vitamin C, vitamin B6, and phytonutrient content in blueberries supports heart health.
Rasberries They are low in calories but high in fiber, vitamins, minerals and antioxidants. They may protect against diabetes, cancer, obesity, arthritis and other conditions and may even provide anti-aging effects.

Greens

Consuming green smoothies or juices are a good way to get vegetables in your diet. Those vegetables include spinach, kale, arugula, and more.
Product Benefits
Spinach An excellent source of vitamin K, vitamin A, vitamin C and folate as well as being a good source of manganese, magnesium, iron and vitamin B2.
Kale High on fiber and water. It contains B vitamins, and vitamin C, which promotes iron absorption.
Romaine Lettuce Packed with vitamin C, vitamin K, and folate. It's a good source of beta carotene, which converts into vitamin A in the body.
Celery A great source of antioxidants. It contains vitamin C, beta carotene, and flavonoids.
Carrots They are a particularly good source of beta carotene, fiber, vitamin K1, potassium, and antioxidants.

Greens

Aside from using fruits, there are loads of healthy add-ins for smoothies that can take help boost your day.
Product Benefits
Almonds Source of vitamin E, copper, magnesium, and high-quality protein. They also contain high levels of healthy unsaturated fatty acids along with high levels of bioactive molecules
Ginger Possible health benefits include relieving nausea, loss of appetite, motion sickness, and pain.
Peanut Butter Relatively low-carbohydrate food that contains good amounts of fats and protein, as well as some fiber.
Whole Grain Oats They're a gluten-free whole grain and a great source of important vitamins, minerals, fiber and antioxidants.
